Chicken Congee

Back to Homepage

Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 60 minutes
Servings: 4

Description

The word "congee" was derived from from the Tamil language of Ancient India, "kanji". In Cantonese, we call it "jÅ«k" (which kinda sounds like "jook"), but there are many, many different variations and names for it across Asia.

Even though congee is commonly known as a rice porridge, it wasn't always the case. With thousands of years of history in China, congee was made with whatever grains were available locally: millet, cornmeal, barley, and etc.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 rice cup white rice (a "rice cup" is about 3/4 of a standard US cup. Jasmine rice is preferred but any white rice will work!)
  • 12 oz chicken
  • 8 cup water
  • 0.50 oz ginger
  • 2 green onions, sliced diagonally into 1/2 inch pieces
  • Cilantro (to taste)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 0.50 oz chicken bouillon

Chicken Marinate

  • 2 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 0.50 tsp chicken bouillon
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Directions

Rice

  1. Wash rice
  2. Bring water to a boil
  3. Pour the rice into the boiling water
  4. Cover the pot and let it cook at medium-high heat for 25 minutes
  5. After 25 minutes, whisk the rice

Chicken Marinade

  1. Add oyster sauce, cornstarch, chicken bouillon, and water
  2. Mix

Chicken

  1. Cut the chicken into thin slices and place it in a bowl to marinate. Mix and massage the chicken and the marinade together for 30 seconds until there's no liquid left

Put Them Together

  • Put the chicken into the boiling rice, slowly, over 60 seconds
  • Stir for 2 minutes
  • Add vegetable oil to the bowl and mix for 20-30 seconds.
  • Add ginger, salt, and chicken bouillon
  • Stir for 30 seconds
    • Serving
    1. Garnish with green onions and cilantro
    2. Serve and enjoy
